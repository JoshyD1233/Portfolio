Unnamed Murder Mystery

Single Player, Murder Mystery Experience

This project was created as part of a research project at university that aimed to answer the question: 'Evaluating Spatial Design and Environmental Storytelling as Narrative Tools in Video Games'. The experience functions similarly to most murder mysteries with the key difference being that dialogue is kept to a minimum. The player needing to utilise interactions with the environment itself to piece together the story and ultimately figure out who commited the murder.

DISCLAIMER: The majority of assets and textures were sourced from Unreal Engine's FAB Store

Planning the Narrative

The first step in creating the murder mystery was to plan both the layout of the narrative and the physical environment. To create the narrative I utilised Twine to plan out how I wanted the story to be experienced from the players perspective. I also created the whitebox alongside this in order to effetively visualise the space.

The Environment

The player starts in the storage cabin with each of the four suspects. From here these suspects can be accused and briefly spoken to in order to provide background context from their points of view to the player. This is only character dialogue in the entire experience.

The next cabin is the lower class cabin. In this section candlelight and uncomfortable tightly compact seating highlights the lack of luxury in the space.

This is the middle class cabin. seating in this space is separated into compartments and the lighting comes from an electrical source instead of candlelight. Clues were able to be more effectively hidden because of this and as a result some of the most important clues are found in this section.

Above is the Upper Class Cabin featuring comfortable and luxurious spaces. This is final area that players explore and as a result contains clues aimed at tying clues found in previous cabins together.

An ariel shot of the Lower, Middle and Upper class cabins.
